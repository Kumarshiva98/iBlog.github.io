The fastest gust of wind ever recorded on Earthwas 253 miles per hour.

Hang on to your hats because this isn't your average wind storm In 1996, a tropical cyclone named Olivia hit off the coast of Barrow Island, Australia with such a force that it broke and incredible record. According to The Weather Channel,"Olivia's eyewall produced five extreme three-second wind gusts, the peak of which was 253 mph gust," Which blew past the previous wind record of 231 mph set in Mount Washington, New Hampshire back in 1934.
